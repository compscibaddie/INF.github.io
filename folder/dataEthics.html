Data Ethics

What is data ethics?

Data ethics refers to the principles behind how an organisations gather, protect and use data

why is data ethics relevant

  1. Building customer trust
    • Customer trust is the foundation of any business
    • By prioritising data ethics, a business acn demonstrate commitment to protecting customer privacy and sercurity
    • This built trust leads to customer loyalty, positive word-of-mouth and enhanced brand reputation
  2. Brand reputation and image
    • Reputation is a very valuable asset to a business
    • Compromising customer data sercurity acn be detrimental to business' reputation due to the fact that customers are attracted to strong brand reputations
  3. Gaining a competitives advantage
    • In today's competitive landscape, data is a valuable differntiator to a business's reputation
    • Coustomers are becoming increasingly concerned about privacy and data sercurity
  4. Fulfilling ethical reponsibilty
    • ethical data practices reflect on an organisation's commitment to upholding societal values and contributing to a responsible data-sriven ecosystem.

where can data ethics be seen in the world of business

  1. Apple
    • the multi-bilion dollar tech company's privacy policiy emphasises minimal collection of customer data, provides transparency reports as well as giving the user a significant amount of control over their data
  2. GDPR
    • The General Data Protection Regulation by the European Union grants individuals to know what data is being collected about them, why it is being collected as well as where it is being stored
    • It requtres organisations to protect personal data and notify authourities of data breaches within 72 hours
  3. Facebook and Cambridge Analytica scandal
    • In 2018 it was discoverd that Cmbridge Analytica had harvested the personal data of millions of Facebook users without their consent which led to a significant fallout leading to the call for stricter data regualtions worldwide

the legal and regulatory aspect of data ethics

Data protection laws and regulations were put in place to ensure that individuals' personal data wasn't being harvested without their consent as this had been a growing problem as the world became more data-driven
These are just some regulations/laws regarding data protection from around the world:
  1. PoPI Act
    • This stands for Protection of Personal Information Act
    • This is South Africa's equivelent to thr EU GDPR, this act protects data subjects from harm,theft and discrimination.
    • This act ensure lawful processing of personal data of individuals
  2. PECR
    • This stands for the Privacy of Electrical Communications Regulations
    • This sets more specific privacy rights on electrical communications
  3. Data Protection Act
    • This act controls how your personal information is used by organisation,businesses or the government.

the consequences of data misuse

Just like an law of a country there are consequences to violating these laws and data protection laws are no exception. These can include criminal prosecution, civil penalties such as fines, as well as destroying a business's brand reputation which will lead to financial loss.
