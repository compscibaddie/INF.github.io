data visualisation

what is data visualisation?

data visualisation simple put is the representation of data through common graphical means such as charts and graphs.

where is it used?

although data visualisation may seem like an industry specific concept, it can be seen in everyday things/places like: fitness trackers, speedometer, workout workout machines and player stats.

getting to know the basics of data visualisation

  1. define your business goals
    one of the first steps in data visualisation is understanding what your business' objectives are in order to identify what problems need to be solved. take into account what vital stratigic question will you be addressing as well as what value does the information you are presenting give to the business.
  2. know your audience
    the data visualisation startegy you choose should be designed in such a way that it would be able to effectively communicate with your target audience.
  3. keep visuals simple
    Simplicity is best when it comes to displaying your selected visuals no matter the audience, because if the visuals are complex and difficult to understand it could result in difficultity in making decisions or recommendations form it's content.
  4. provide context
    consider connecting your data to a broader concept and not just presenting the data. Provide answers to questions such as what does it mean for the problem/subject being addressed?
  5. make it doable
    one of the most vital steps is to ensure that the data you are sharing is actionable as the best data visualisations turn insights into action.

the benefits of data visualisation

  1. Helps with making better business decisions
    • data visualisation tools enable effortless creation of intuitive and interactive visualisations
    • data visualisation helps with easily identifying key insights on business ventures
    • having a data visualisation tool can enhance collaboration and communication in the decision making approach
    • it also significantly reduces imprecision in data
  2. Easier trend analysis
    • data visualisation helps to analyse current and historical events and predict future events such as potential growth or decline in sales and/or revenue
  3. Identifying connections between operations and faster results
    • identifying connections and relationships between indepentant variables helps you to stay ahead of competition,track correlations between operations and overall business performance in order to effectively make more important strategic decisions.
  4. accurate analysis of customer preferences
    • data visualisation allows you to understand the preferences of your target audience
    • data visualisation tools enables you to utilise the customer analysis in order to create better business strategies
