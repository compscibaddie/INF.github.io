Information Quality

the basics of information quality

Information Quality refers to the completeness, accuracy,reliabilty and relevence of data and information that businesses rely on for decision making.

the importance and benefits of prioritising information quality

  1. Prioritising Information quality ensures that the business can analyse data, conduct marketing campaigns as well as creating reliable strategies this will result in a better return on investment.
  2. it also reduces costs risks and worker productivity as quality data, marketers and data manager spend more time on using the data for it's intended purpose.
  3. Quality data can also help businesses engage with customers more effectively, ensuring that those in the system are valid, active contacts. It can even help avoid brand damage.

poor information quality

Poor informationquality is detrimental a business. These are just some of the ways:
  • loss of income:
    losing clients andc potential clients affects the revenue of a business. poor information quality in the form of inaccurate capturing of client details or misinformation of a product
  • inaccurate analysis:
    When data analysis is based on incomplete and inaccurate data it risks misinterpretation as well as poor decision making
  • Missed oppotunities:
    without high information quality to base your decisions on, your business will miss out on oppotunities like market trends, customer insight and product improvements.
